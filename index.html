Popular Destinations

Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple

The Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple, located in Pune, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Established over 125 years ago by Dagdusheth Halwai, a sweetmaker, the temple is renowned for its stunning Ganesh idol adorned with gold and jewelry. It is a major spiritual and cultural landmark, especially during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival, when thousands of devotees gather to celebrate. The temple is also known for its charitable activities and social contributions, making it a symbol of devotion and community service in Pune.

Shaniwar wada

Shaniwar Wada, located in Pune, is a historic fort built in 1732 by Bajirao I, the Prime Minister of the Maratha Empire. It served as the political center of the Marathas and is known for its grand architecture, including the iconic Delhi Gate and intricate wooden carvings. Despite being largely destroyed by a fire in 1828, its ruins remain a popular tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into Maratha history. The fort is also associated with legends and stories, including the tragic tale of Narayanrao Peshwa, adding a mystical allure to its historical significance.

Aga Khan Palace

The Aga Khan Palace, located in Pune, India, is a historic landmark built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III. It is known for its beautiful architecture and lush gardens. The palace holds significant historical importance as it served as a place of detention for Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, and other freedom fighters during India’s struggle for independence. Kasturba Gandhi passed away here in 1944, and a memorial in her honor is present on the premises. Today, the palace is a museum showcasing artifacts and photographs related to Gandhi’s life and the Indian freedom movement, attracting visitors and history enthusiasts.

Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort, located near Pune, is a historic hilltop fort with great significance in Maratha history. Originally known as Kondhana, it was renamed after Shivaji Maharaj's forces, led by Tanaji Malusare, recaptured it in 1670 in a legendary battle. The fort stands as a symbol of bravery and sacrifice, with Tanaji's heroic efforts remembered as "the fort was won, but the lion was lost." Situated at an elevation of around 1,312 meters, the fort offers stunning views and attracts visitors for trekking, historical exploration, and its natural beauty.

Pataleshwar Cave Temple

The Pataleshwar Cave Temple, located in Pune, is a rock-cut cave temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Believed to date back to the 8th century, it was carved out of a single basalt rock and features intricate carvings and a serene atmosphere. The temple includes a main shrine housing a Shiva Lingam and a circular pavilion with a massive Nandi idol. Known for its historical and architectural significance, the temple offers a peaceful retreat amidst the bustling city, attracting history enthusiasts and devotees alike.

Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum

The Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, located in Pune, is a treasure trove of art and artifacts collected by Dr. Dinkar G. Kelkar. Established in 1962, the museum showcases over 20,000 items, including sculptures, musical instruments, textiles, and everyday objects from different parts of India. The highlight is the Mastani Mahal, a reconstructed section of the palace of Mastani, the wife of Bajirao I. The museum offers a glimpse into India’s rich cultural heritage and craftsmanship, making it a must-visit for history and art enthusiasts.

Parvati Hill

Parvati Hill, located in Pune, is a historic and scenic spot known for its temples and panoramic views of the city. Atop the hill stands the Parvati Temple, dedicated to Goddess Parvati, along with temples for Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and Kartikeya. Built during the Peshwa era in the 18th century, the hill has great historical significance as it was a place of worship for the Peshwas. Visitors climb 103 steps to reach the top, where they can enjoy a peaceful atmosphere, stunning views, and a small museum showcasing artifacts from the Peshwa period.

Osho Ashram

The Osho Ashram, also known as Osho International Meditation Resort, is located in Koregaon Park, Pune. It was founded by spiritual leader Osho (Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh) and serves as a global center for meditation and personal growth. The ashram offers a serene environment with lush gardens, meditation halls, and modern facilities for visitors to practice various meditation techniques, including Osho's dynamic meditation. It attracts people from all over the world seeking spiritual transformation and relaxation. The ashram's unique blend of spirituality and modernity makes it a notable destination in Pune.

Pune-Okayama Friendship Garden

The Pune-Okayama Friendship Garden, also known as Pu La Deshpande Garden, is a beautifully designed Japanese-style garden located in Pune. Inspired by the Korakuen Garden in Okayama, Japan, it symbolizes the friendship between the two cities. Spread over 10 acres, the garden features lush greenery, serene water streams, colorful flowers, and traditional Japanese landscaping elements. It offers a peaceful retreat for visitors, making it a popular spot for relaxation, photography, and family outings. The garden's tranquil ambiance and aesthetic appeal make it a cherished landmark in Pune.

Khadakwasla Dam

Khadakwasla Dam, located near Pune, is an important water reservoir built on the Mutha River. Constructed in 1869, the dam serves as a vital source of water supply for Pune city and surrounding areas. The dam is also known for its picturesque surroundings, making it a popular spot for picnics and nature lovers. The backwaters of the dam are scenic and provide a peaceful retreat. Additionally, Khadakwasla is home to the National Defense Academy (NDA), which adds to the area's significance. The dam plays a crucial role in irrigation and maintaining the local ecosystem.

Pashan Lake

Pashan Lake, located in Pune, is a scenic artificial lake that serves as a key water source for the surrounding areas. It is known for its serene atmosphere and lush surroundings, making it a popular spot for birdwatching, nature walks, and photography. The lake is home to various species of birds, especially during the migratory season, attracting wildlife enthusiasts. In addition to its natural beauty, Pashan Lake also plays an important role in the local ecosystem, contributing to groundwater recharge. It is a peaceful escape from the city's hustle, offering a calm retreat for visitors.

Vetal Tekdi

Vetal Tekdi, located in Pune, is a popular hilltop that offers panoramic views of the city. It is one of the highest points in Pune and is known for its lush greenery, making it a favorite destination for nature lovers and hikers. The hill is home to various species of birds and plants, making it an ideal spot for birdwatching and trekking. A small temple dedicated to Vetal, a local deity, sits atop the hill, adding cultural significance. Vetal Tekdi is also known for its peaceful atmosphere, providing a perfect escape from the urban environment.

National War Memorial Southern Command

The National War Memorial Southern Command, located in Pune, is a significant tribute to the soldiers of the Indian Army who have sacrificed their lives in various conflicts. The memorial, dedicated to the Southern Command of the Indian Army, honors the bravery and sacrifices of the armed forces, with a focus on those who served in the southern regions of India. The memorial features a striking design with plaques bearing the names of martyrs, as well as sculptures and symbols reflecting military valor. It serves as an important place of remembrance and pays homage to the contributions of India's soldiers.

Chaturshringi Temple

Chaturshringi Temple, located on the slopes of a hill in Pune, is one of the city's most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Goddess Chaturshringi, a form of Goddess Durga. The temple, dating back to the 18th century, is known for its beautiful architecture and serene setting. The temple complex has 108 steps leading to the main shrine, offering visitors a peaceful climb with views of the surrounding area. The deity, Chaturshringi, is believed to fulfill the wishes of devotees, making it an important spiritual site. The temple attracts a large number of pilgrims and tourists, especially during festivals.

Mulshi Lake and Dam

Mulshi Lake and Dam, located near Pune, are significant both for their scenic beauty and utility. The dam, built on the Mula River in the 1920s, serves as a crucial water source for irrigation and power generation in the region. The surrounding Mulshi Lake, with its picturesque landscape, is a popular destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers. The area is also known for its serene environment, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and picnics. The Mulshi Dam plays an important role in sustaining the local ecosystem and providing water to nearby agricultural areas.

Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park

The Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park, located in Pune, is one of the prominent zoological parks in India. Spread over 130 acres, the park is home to a wide variety of animals, birds, and reptiles, including tigers, lions, elephants, and numerous species of birds. It aims to promote wildlife conservation and education through its well-maintained enclosures, animal rescue programs, and awareness initiatives. The zoo also features a large lake, a safari area, and a snake park, making it a popular destination for families, wildlife enthusiasts, and school groups. Its efforts in animal rehabilitation and conservation make it an important institution in Pune.

Katraj Lake

Katraj Lake, located in the southern part of Pune, is a serene and popular spot for nature lovers and families. Situated near the Katraj Snake Park, the lake is known for its picturesque surroundings, clean waters, and lush greenery. It serves as an ideal location for picnics, evening walks, and birdwatching, with several species of birds often seen around the area. The lake is also a key water reservoir for the city, contributing to the local ecosystem. Its peaceful atmosphere and proximity to other attractions make Katraj Lake a popular recreational destination in Pune.

Empress Garden

Empress Garden, located in Pune, is a beautiful and historic botanical garden spread over 39 acres. Established in 1856 by the British, it is one of the oldest gardens in the city and is known for its wide variety of plants, flowers, and lush greenery. The garden features well-maintained pathways, a pond, and several rare species of plants, making it a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and a popular spot for walks and relaxation. Empress Garden also hosts seasonal flower shows, attracting visitors from around the region, making it a key landmark in Pune's green spaces.

Bund Garden

Bund Garden, located near the Pune Cantonment, is a scenic and historic garden that offers a peaceful retreat in the city. Built in the early 19th century by the British, it is situated along the Mula River and is known for its well-maintained pathways, lush greenery, and beautiful views of the river. The garden is a popular spot for jogging, walking, and picnicking, attracting both locals and tourists. It also features a boat club for recreational boating. Bund Garden serves as an important green space in the urban landscape, providing a serene escape from the city's hustle and bustle.

ISKCON NVCC Temple

The ISKCON NVCC Temple, located in Pune, is a significant religious and cultural center for devotees of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON). Dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha, the temple is known for its beautiful architecture, serene atmosphere, and vibrant spiritual activities. It offers a peaceful place for meditation, prayer, and learning about Vedic teachings. The temple also hosts cultural events, bhajans (devotional songs), and festivals like Janmashtami, attracting a large number of devotees. The ISKCON NVCC Temple is not only a religious landmark but also a center for promoting spirituality and community welfare.

Joshi's Museum of Miniature Railways

Joshi's Museum of Miniature Railways, located in Pune, is a unique and fascinating museum dedicated to showcasing miniature models of trains and railway stations. The museum, established in 1998, is the brainchild of Shree S. B. Joshi, an enthusiast of model railways. It features an impressive collection of meticulously crafted miniature trains, tracks, and landscapes, offering a detailed representation of various railway systems from around the world. The museum's intricate displays, combined with working models of trains that run on the tracks, make it a captivating experience for visitors of all ages, particularly train enthusiasts and families.

Shinde Chhatri

Shinde Chhatri, located in Pune, is a historical monument dedicated to the Maratha warrior and general, Mahadji Shinde. Built in the 19th century, it is a stunning blend of Maratha and Mughal architectural styles, featuring intricate carvings, arches, and a large dome. The memorial houses a cenotaph (chhatri) honoring Shinde, who played a crucial role in the Maratha Empire's revival in the late 18th century. Shinde Chhatri is also known for its peaceful atmosphere and is a significant cultural landmark in Pune, attracting both history enthusiasts and tourists.

Lal Mahal

Lal Mahal, located in Pune, is a historic palace that holds great significance in Maratha history. Built in the 17th century by Shahaji Bhosale, the father of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, it was the birthplace of Shivaji's son, Sambhaji. The palace is known for its vibrant red walls, which give it its name ("Lal" meaning red in Marathi). Though the original structure was destroyed in the 19th century, the current monument is a reconstruction that houses a museum showcasing artifacts related to Shivaji Maharaj and the Maratha Empire. Lal Mahal is a symbol of Maratha heritage and attracts numerous visitors interested in India's rich history.

Baner Hill

Baner Hill, located in Pune, is a popular hilltop offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding areas. Known for its natural beauty, the hill is a favorite spot for trekkers, nature lovers, and photographers. It is also home to various species of flora and fauna, making it an ideal location for birdwatching and nature walks. The area is well-known for its cool climate, especially during the summer months, and provides a peaceful escape from the city's hustle. Baner Hill is a serene retreat for those seeking outdoor activities and relaxation amidst nature.

Taljai Hills

Taljai Hills, located in Pune, is a popular hillock known for its scenic beauty and tranquil environment. Situated near the Taljai Forest, the area is rich in flora and fauna, making it a great spot for nature enthusiasts and birdwatchers. The hill offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes, with well-maintained walking trails that attract fitness enthusiasts, joggers, and trekkers. Taljai Hills is also home to a small temple dedicated to Taljai Devi, adding a spiritual element to the serene surroundings. It is a peaceful retreat for those looking to escape the city's hustle and enjoy the outdoors.

Pu La Deshpande Garden

Pu La Deshpande Garden, located in Pune, is a beautiful and well-maintained public garden dedicated to the renowned Marathi writer and humorist, Pu La Deshpande. Spread over a large area, the garden is known for its lush greenery, serene atmosphere, and vibrant flowerbeds. It features various sections, including a musical fountain, a children's play area, and walking tracks, making it a popular spot for families, joggers, and nature lovers. The garden's peaceful ambiance and well-planned layout offer a perfect escape for relaxation and recreation, making it one of Pune's cherished green spaces.

Blades of Glory Cricket Museum

Blades of Glory Cricket Museum, located in Pune, is a unique museum dedicated to the rich history of cricket. It showcases a vast collection of cricket memorabilia, including bats, balls, jerseys, and signed items from legendary cricketers such as Sachin Tendulkar, Brian Lara, and Donald Bradman. The museum is designed to take visitors on a journey through the evolution of the game, with exhibits covering major milestones, historical events, and the achievements of iconic players. It is a must-visit for cricket enthusiasts, offering an immersive experience that celebrates the sport's legacy and its most significant moments.

Fergusson College

Fergusson College, located in Pune, is one of the oldest and most prestigious educational institutions in India. Established in 1885, it is affiliated with Savitribai Phule Pune University and offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate courses in arts, science, and commerce. Known for its rich history and academic excellence, the college has produced many notable alumni, including scholars, politicians, and artists. The campus, with its beautiful British-era architecture, lush green surroundings, and state-of-the-art facilities, provides an ideal environment for learning and intellectual growth. Fergusson College continues to be a leading institution in Pune, known for its academic rigor and contribution to higher education.

Symbiosis International University

Symbiosis International University (SIU), located in Pune, is one of India’s top private universities, known for its academic excellence and global perspective. Established in 2002, SIU offers a wide range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across diverse fields, including management, law, engineering, health sciences, and media. The university has a reputation for providing world-class education and promoting international collaborations, with students from various countries. SIU is known for its state-of-the-art campus, experienced faculty, and emphasis on research and innovation, making it a leading institution for higher education in India.

Saras baug

Saras Baug is a popular and peaceful garden located in Pune, Maharashtra. It is known for its lush greenery, well-maintained lawns, and a temple dedicated to Lord Ganesh at the center. The temple, called the **Sarasbaug Ganapati Temple**, is one of the oldest and most revered in the city. The garden is spread over 25 acres and is a favorite spot for morning walks, picnics, and relaxation. It also has a picturesque pond and is a significant cultural landmark in Pune. The area is particularly busy during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists. The serene ambiance and natural beauty make Saras Baug a must-visit place for anyone visiting Pune.
